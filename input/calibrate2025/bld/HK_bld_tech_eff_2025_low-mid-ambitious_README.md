# HK building technology efficiency scenarios for 2025

## Files and scope

The three cases alter only the technical efficiency of **2025 new building-service technology vintages** in Hong Kong. They do not retrofit the entire 2021 stock, alter service demand, change technology costs or share-weights, or modify building-shell conductance. Residential deciles d1-d10 receive identical values.

## Scenario interpretation

- **Conservative:** compliance-led and gradual equipment replacement. It uses smaller improvements than the central case but does not deliberately reduce any technology below its GCAM default 2025 s-curve value.
- **Central:** the previously prepared policy-calibrated case.
- **Ambitious:** rapid uptake of high-efficiency air conditioning, LED lighting, labelled appliances, advanced controls and best-available equipment in new 2025 vintages.

The assumed intensity ratio is energy input per unit service in 2025 divided by the corresponding 2005 value. GCAM efficiency is service output divided by energy input, so `efficiency_2025 = efficiency_2005 / intensity_ratio`.

## Core intensity ratios: 2025 / 2005

| End use / technology | Conservative | Central | Ambitious |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Residential ordinary cooling | 0.75 | 0.65 | 0.55 |
| Residential high-efficiency cooling | 0.82 | 0.72 | 0.62 |
| Commercial ordinary cooling | 0.80 | 0.72 | 0.62 |
| Commercial high-efficiency cooling | 0.86 | 0.80 | 0.70 |
| Residential lighting | 0.72 | 0.60 | 0.48 |
| Commercial ordinary lighting | 0.75 | 0.65 | 0.50 |
| Commercial fluorescent lighting | 0.85 | 0.75 | 0.62 |
| Residential and commercial appliances | 0.88 | 0.82 | 0.74 |
| Commercial gas hot water/cooking, ordinary | 0.90 | 0.82 | 0.74 |
| Residential gas/fuel hot water/cooking, ordinary | 0.92 | 0.85 | 0.75 |
| Commercial heat pump | 0.85 | 0.75 | 0.65 |

Resistance heating, high-efficiency resistance water heating, district heat, and technical placeholder technologies remain at or near 1.00 where additional conversion-efficiency gains are not well supported.

## Important interpretation

A 0.55 intensity ratio does not mean the whole building stock uses 45% less energy in 2025. It applies only to the service energy requirement of the corresponding **new 2025 technology vintage**. Stock turnover, technology shares, costs, floor area, income, climate and service demand determine the aggregate building-energy result.
